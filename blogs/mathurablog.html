 Nidhivan Temple

This temple is one of the most religious temples in Mathura. The locals, tourists, and visitors have a belief that the spirit of Lord Krishna resides in it. In the evening, the gates of the temple are closed because of a saying that Lord Krishna performs Rasleela with his gopis. No one is allowed to visit the temple after the gates are shut, and if one does, the person may turn, blind, deaf, and dumb. The temple is surrounded by trees and forests that provide a majestic view of the temple.

Dwarkadheesh Temple

Lord Krishna is known by the name of Dwarkadheesh, which means the emperor of the city of Dwarka. Dwarkadheesh Temple Mathura is a well-built example of brilliant architecture done in the Rajasthani form. It is believed to be one of the best temples in Mathura. During festivals, the temple is packed by the flocks of devotees and pilgrims from all around the world. With the shrine of Lord Krishna and Goddess Radha, Dwarkadheesh Temple Mathura also has black idols of Lord Krishna and white marble idol of Goddess Radha.

Shri Krishna Janmabhoomi Temple

The city of Mathura always remains crowded with pilgrims visiting the best temples in Mathura. Since it is the birthplace of Lord Krishna, this temple has a distinctive relevance for the devotees. It is believed to be the holiest shrines among all the shrines of the deity in the city. There is a small room behind the temple, which is considered sacred. The locals believe that it is the same prison of King Kans in which Lord Krishna was born.
